grahamBot

July 2020 - September 2020

I developed grahamBot because checking to see if a stock passed basic quality tests, like say, a 33% increase in earnings over the last 10 years, was getting old fast. I needed a way to filter out the proverbial wheat from the chaff.

grahamBot allows you to quickly check if a stock (or an excel list of stock names) is worth researching more extensively. To do this, it extracts stock information from the internet (also known as web scraping) and uses 12 algorithms based on the recommendations outlined in the classic value investing text The Intelligent Investor by Benjamin Graham (mentor of Warren Buffet).

In the end grahamBot presents a report detailing the results of the tests, as well as the names of any stocks that passed all the tests.

Programmed using Python3, Pandas, and Scrapy

Screen printing image

Carlson's Custom Apparel

Founder & CEO, since Fall 2018

Hockessin, DE

Grasping for an opportunity to enter the world of entrepreneurship, I happened to overhear a conversation in my homeroom of high school. Our grade needed a new supplier for its t-shirts at the upcoming pep rally. Equipped with a nugget of knowledge on the t-shirt printing process and a contact (who I thought was in the industry), I decided I was ripe for the job.

Little did I know that I would instead end up printing a different class's shirts, and eventually be responsible for the printing of ~300 shirts throughout the next year. In that year I would learn the ins and outs of the screen printing process, and develop a reputation for simple, inexpensive custom t-shirts.

While the company continues to target the high school, collegiate, local markets, the pandemic has led me to believe a pivot and rebranding of the company is needed in the direction of a more premium, upscale online presence. This change is currently underway.
